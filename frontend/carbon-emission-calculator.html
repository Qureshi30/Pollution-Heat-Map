Understanding Carbon Footprint

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions. The average carbon footprint for a person in the United States is 16 tons, one of the highest rates in the world.

Carbon footprints are caused by:

  • Transportation (cars, planes, ships, etc.)
  • Electricity usage
  • Food production (especially meat and dairy)
  • Manufacturing of goods
  • Building heating and cooling

Reducing your carbon footprint is crucial for combating climate change. Even small changes in daily habits can make a significant difference when adopted by many people.
